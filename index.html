Details

Initial results from our study indicate that North Carolina is rich with underutilized biomass energy resources. In more rural areas, residues from animal and crop-based agriculture provide a notable oppurtunity for energy production. And, in more urban counties, the presence of large amounts of organic municipal waste highlight pathways for converting waste into biomass energy byproducts. Lastly, residues from forestry operations across the state represent a notable, although more diffuse, potential resource.
